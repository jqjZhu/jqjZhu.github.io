The horsepower and mpg of a car show a negative correlation trend.

Moreover, American car brands generally have higher horsepower, and the number of kilometers per gallon is lower than that of Japanese auto industry brands and European brands, and they are generally poor in terms of fuel efficiency.

Between 1970 and 1980, Japanese auto industry brands accounted for 20%, while American brands accounted for 63%.

Looking at the trend over time, the Mileage per gallon performances of the overall car brand is showing an upward trend.

From the comparison of the number of cylinders, it can be said that the more the number of cylinders, the higher the fuel efficiency.
